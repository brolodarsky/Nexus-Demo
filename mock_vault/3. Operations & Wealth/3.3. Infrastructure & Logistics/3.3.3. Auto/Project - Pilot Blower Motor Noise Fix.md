This guide covers the diagnosis and resolution of unusual noises (rattling, squeaking, grinding) coming from the blower motor in a 2005 Honda Pilot.

## Overview
In the 2005 Honda Pilot, blower motor noise is most commonly caused by debris (leaves, dust, rodent nests) trapped in the squirrel cage fan. Cleaning the fan often restores quiet operation without needing a full replacement.

## Diagnostic Steps
- **Symptom:** Rattling or vibrating that increases with fan speed.
    - **Likely Cause:** Debris inside the squirrel cage fan unbalancing the motor.
- **Symptom:** High-pitched squealing or grinding.
    - **Likely Cause:** Worn bearings within the motor assembly.
- **Symptom:** No air flow or fan only works on high.
    - **Likely Cause:** Faulty blower motor resistor (usually separate from the motor noise issue).

## Repair Procedure

### 1. Access the Blower Motor
- The assembly is located under the passenger side dashboard, behind the glovebox.
- Remove the plastic trim panel under the glovebox by detaching the clips or screws.
- Locate the circular blower motor housing.

### 2. Removal
- Disconnect the electrical power connector from the motor.
- Remove the three 8mm screws (or Phillips head, depending on replacement history) holding the motor in place.
- Carefully lower the motor assembly from the HVAC housing.

### 3. Inspection and Cleaning
- Inspect the **squirrel cage** (fan blades) for leaves, twigs, or other foreign objects.
- Use a vacuum or compressed air to remove loose debris.
- If the blades are coated in dust, clean them with a brush and damp cloth (ensure the motor itself stays dry).
- **Tip:** Vacuum out the opening where the motor was mounted to ensure no more debris falls in.

### 4. Testing & Reinstallation
- Plug the motor back in while holding it securely and momentarily turn the fan on to check for vibration or noise.
- If quiet, reinstall by reversing the removal steps.
- If it still squeals or grinds after cleaning, the motor is likely failing and should be replaced.

## Prevention
- **[[Cabin Air Filter]]:** Replace the cabin air filter every 15,000 miles to prevent debris from entering the HVAC system.
