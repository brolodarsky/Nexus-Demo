## 1. Knowledge Base & Concepts
*Core mechanical understanding of the 2005 Honda Pilot (1st Generation, 3.5L J35A4 Engine).*

### Powertrain & Engine (J35 V6)
- **Timing Belt & Water Pump**: The J35 is an *interference engine*. The timing belt, water pump, pulleys, and hydraulic tensioner must be replaced every 105,000 miles or 7 years. Failure causes catastrophic engine damage.
- **Valve Clearances**: Solid lifters require periodic adjustment (typically during the 105k service). A loud tick means loose valves (annoying but safe); quiet valves might be overly tight (dangerous, can burn exhaust valves).
- **EGR System**: The EGR valve and intake manifold ports frequently suffer from carbon buildup, causing rough idling, hesitation, and P0401/P0404 OBD2 codes.
- **Oil Life**: Always use 5W-20. Due to age, check oil levels every 1,000 miles between changes as the J35 can consume a small amount of oil as rings wear.

### Transmission & Drivetrain
- **ATF Drain & Fill**: The 5-speed automatic is sensitive. *Never* use a pressurized fluid flush machine. Use the "3x Drain and Fill" method exclusively using Honda ATF DW-1 (which superseded the original ATF-Z1). 
- **VTM-4 Rear Differential (4WD)**: Requires specialized Honda VTM-4 fluid (approx. 3 quarts). Change every 30,000 miles to prevent groaning or "juddering" noises during tight low-speed turns.

### Suspension, Chassis & Common Quirks
- **Compliance Bushings**: The front lower control arm compliance (rearward) bushings are known to tear, leading to sloppy steering feel and a clunk when braking.
- **HVAC Blower Motor**: The under-dash blower AC motor bearing often wears out, causing a high-pitched "cricket" chirping noise. (See your active [[Project - Pilot Blower Motor Noise Fix]]).
