## 1. Preparation & Foundation (Rookie Protocols)
* **The "Pre-Flight" Clean:** Before removing the intake manifold, blow off the top of the engine with compressed air (or brush it down). Dirt falling into an open engine cylinder is catastrophic.
* **Disconnect Negative Battery Terminal:** Wait 15 mins for the system to discharge.
* **The "Tape & Sharpie" Method:** Buy a multipack of colored painters tape. Use Blue for coolant hoses, Red for vacuum lines, and Yellow for electrical connectors. Put a matching numbered piece of tape on both the hose and the port it connects to.
* **Ziploc Bag System:** Store bolts for specific components in individual, labeled bags. Never mix bolts from different steps.
* **The "Bolt Map":** For components with many bolts of different lengths, poke the bolts through a piece of cardboard in the same pattern they were removed.
* **Photo Documentation:** Take high-resolution photos *before* you touch anything. Take more photos as you remove layers. 
* **The "Frustration Protocol":** You *will* drop a bolt. A brittle 15-year-old plastic connector *will* snap. When you feel the urge to pull aggressively, step away for 10 minutes. Mistakes multiply when you're tired or angry.
* **Stuck Bolts:** Use **PB Blaster** for at least 15-30 mins.
* **Assembly:** Hand-thread bolts first for 3-4 turns. "Lefty-Loosey, Righty-Tighty."

## 2. Phase 2: Major Engine Service (Top End & Timing)
**Symptoms:** P0128 (Cold/Hot), Smoke/Burning Smell, P0325 (Jolting), Preventative Maintenance (Timing).
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **This is the main event.** Printing this section is highly recommended. The Knock Sensor, Valve Cover Gaskets, and Spark Plugs all share the same massive labor bottleneck: **The Intake Manifold.** We are also adding the **Timing Belt & Water Pump** to this phase, as draining the coolant for the thermostat makes it the perfect time to tackle the water pump.
### **Master Tools Needed for Phase 2**
- **Sockets:** 8mm, 10mm, 12mm, 24mm deep socket (specifically for Knock Sensor).
- **Wrenches:** 10mm wrench, extensions (3", 6" essential for intake bolts).
- **Torque Wrench:** Mandatory for Intake Manifold (16 ft-lb) and Knock Sensor (23 ft-lb/31 Nm). High-capacity torque wrench needed for Crank Pulley (181 ft-lbs).
- **Specialty:** Spark Plug socket (5/8"), 50mm Crank Pulley Tool, Pliers, Trim Tools.
- **Supplies:** Silicone paste (for tube seals), Brake Parts cleaner, Rags, RTV Sealant (use sparingly only on corners), Ziploc bags for bolts.
- **Vision & Rescue:** A powerful Headlamp/Magnetic Hood Light, and a Telescoping Magnetic Pick-up Tool for dropped bolts.

> [!TIP] **Master Torque Spec Cheat Sheet**
> Print this and keep it next to your wrench!
> * **Spark Plugs:** 13 ft-lbs (18 Nm)
> * **Valve Cover Bolts:** 8.8 ft-lbs (12 Nm) — *Do not overtighten!*
> * **Crankcase Pulley Bolt:** 181 ft-lbs (245 Nm) — *Requires serious leverage.*
> * **Intake Plenum & Injector Bases (Runners):** 16 ft-lbs (22 Nm) — *Tighten in a star pattern.*
> * **Knock Sensor:** 23 ft-lbs (31 Nm)

### **Job 4.5: Reinstall Front Injector Base (Runner) - The Debris Shield**
**Strategic Advantage:** By bolting the Front Injector Base back down *now*, you seal up the front cylinders. Because we brilliantly never removed the Rear Injector Base, the rear cylinders are automatically sealed. This completely shields the engine head intake ports from flying rubber bits when you aggressively scrape the valve covers in the next steps.
- [x] **Reinstall Front Injector Base (Runner):** Place the new intake manifold gasket down on the perfectly clean front cylinder head. Gently lower the Front Injector Base into place. 
- [x] **Torque Base Bolts:** Hand-thread the 12mm bolts. Torque them in the 3-step sequence (**8 ft-lbs, 12 ft-lbs, 16 ft-lbs**) starting from the middle and moving outward. 
- [x] **Cover ALL Holes:** Place clean pieces of cardboard over the top holes of BOTH the front and rear Injector Bases, and tape them down tightly. Your entire engine is now protected from gasket-scraping debris!

### **Job 5: Rear Valve/Cylinder Head Cover Gaskets & Spark Plugs**
**Resources:** 
[TRQ - How to Replace Front & Rear Valve Cover Gaskets 2003-2008 Honda Pilot](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jWWM2nuj2jw&list=PPSV)
- [ ] **Clearance:** Disconnect the ground strap, power steering hose bracket, and various harness retainers to gain sufficient clearance for the rear cover.
- [x] Remove the single 10mm/Torx bolt holding each Ignition Coil. Pull coils out. REPLACE all 6.
- [ ] Remove the five 10mm screws holding the rear Valve Cover. Tap gently with a rubber mallet to break the 15-year-old seal.
> [!WARNING] **Spark Plug Install**
> When installing the new plugs with the 5/8" socket, **DO NOT use anti-seize** on modern NGK or Denso plugs. Their threads are pre-coated. Adding anti-seize will cause you to overtighten and strip the aluminum engine block threads. Torque to exactly **13 ft-lbs**.
- [x] **Spark Plugs:** While cover is off, replace the 3 rear firewall spark plugs.
- [ ] **Clean Mating Surfaces:** *Crucial Step*. Do not rush this.
	1. **Scrape:** Use **PLASTIC** razor blades to carefully peel away the old gasket material and old RTV from the corners. NEVER use metal scrapers or hard sandpaper, as gouging the soft aluminum causes permanent oil leaks.
	2. **Scrub:** Spray brake cleaner onto a rag or a nylon/brass brush, then scrub the stubborn residual rubber bonded to the head. 
	3. **Wipe Outward:** Do a final wipe with a clean brake-cleaner soaked rag. Always wipe *outward* away from the camshafts so dirty debris doesn't fall into the open engine.
- [ ] **Tube Seals:** Pry out old spark plug tube seals. Lubricate new ones with silicone paste and tap in flush using a 30mm socket or seal driver.
- [ ] **RTV Silicone:** Apply a tiny dab of RTV *only* to the sharp corners where the castings meet. Do not coat the rest of the gasket.
- [ ] Install new rubber gasket into the cover's channel, place cover on head, and torque the five bolts to **12 Nm (8.8 ft-lbs)**. *Do not overtighten, these bolts are designed to bottom out.*

### **Job 7: VTEC Spool Valve/Solenoid Gaskets (Oil Leak Fix)**
**Resources:** [leaking oil filter adapter repair V6 Honda](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1z6DqHLr5Rc&list=PPSV)
> [!NOTE] **Efficiency Note**
> This job and Job 8 (Timing Belt) are grouped together as a single "car lifted" session. Jack the car up once, do both jobs, then lower it.

> [!WARNING] **Year-Specific Location**
> Many YouTube guides show the VTEC solenoid high up on the passenger side of the rear cylinder head (above the alternator). That is the **2006–2008 2nd gen** location (J35A9). On the **2005 1st gen** J35A4, the spool valve assembly is on the **rear of the engine block, lower down near the oil filter.** You will likely need to access it from below or from the side — not from above.

**Location (2005 J35A4):** Rear of the engine block, lower section, near the oil filter housing.
- [ ] **Access:** Jack up the car and secure on jack stands. Locate the spool valve assembly on the rear of the block near the oil filter. You may need to remove the splash shield or work from below for clearance.
- [ ] **Remove Solenoid:** Disconnect the electrical connector. Carefully remove the three 10mm bolts holding the VTEC spool valve assembly to the engine block without damaging the alignment dowels.
- [ ] **Clean & Replace:** Remove the old flattened rubber gasket. Clean the mating surface methodically: first use a plastic scraper to remove any large chunks of carbon or old gasket. Then, spray brake cleaner onto a shop rag and scrub the surface with a nylon or soft brass brush until bare metal. *(Note: Since you bought the genuine OEM gasket kit, you do not need to transfer the old screen like some aftermarket kits require).*
- [ ] **Install New:** Lubricate the new main **Filter Assy., Spool Valve (15825-P8A-A01)** and the smaller **Gasket, Solenoid Base (36172-P8A-A01)** with fresh motor oil so they seat without binding. Place them directly into the grooves on the block assembly.
- [ ] **Assemble & Torque:** Hand-tighten the bolts to ensure the dowels seat. Tighten them in an even, cross-pattern to apply uniform pressure. Torque to **72 in-lbs (6 ft-lbs)**. *Do not over-tighten, or you will crush the new gaskets.*
* *Goal:* Stop the oil leak from the spool valve gaskets. Because this is on the lower rear of the block (not above the alternator like the 2nd gen), the leak likely drips downward onto the subframe/exhaust rather than directly onto electrical components — but it still needs sealing.

### **Job 8: Timing Belt & Water Pump (Aisin Kit)**
**Resources:** [TRQ - Honda J35 Timing Belt Replacement](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8yMD1W-FgXk&list=PPSV)

> [!IMPORTANT]
> This is a **zero-tolerance job** because the J35 is an **interference engine** — if the timing is off by even one tooth, the pistons will destroy the valves on first start. Since coolant is already drained (Job 1), it's the perfect time for the water pump.

> [!TIP] **Job 8 Torque Specs**
> * **Water Pump Bolts:** 9 ft-lbs
> * **Timing Belt Tensioner Mounting Bolts (10mm):** 9 ft-lbs
> * **Tensioner Adjuster Pulley Bolt (14mm):** 18 ft-lbs
> * **Idler Pulley Bolt (14mm):** 33 ft-lbs *(with blue threadlocker!)*
> * **Crank Bolt (19mm):** 48 ft-lbs + 60° *(torque-angle method)*
> * **Engine Mount Bracket-to-Block:** 33 ft-lbs
> * **Engine Mount Rubber-to-Bracket:** 32 ft-lbs
> * **Serpentine Belt Tensioner Pulley (14mm):** 33 ft-lbs
> * **Serpentine Belt Tensioner Bottom Bolt:** 16 ft-lbs
> * **Power Steering Pump Bolts:** 16 ft-lbs

#### Step 1: Drain & Prep Access
- [x] **Drain Radiator:** Open bottom petcock screw and remove radiator cap to drain faster. Ensure system is cold — a hot system is pressurized and will spray. Have a drain bucket ready.
- [x] **Disconnect Battery:** 10mm socket — negative first, then positive.
- [x] **Remove Engine Cover & Coolant Reservoir:** Pull coolant reservoir straight up out of its bracket. Pull the line out of its plastic stay. Move the reservoir to the opposite side, out of the way.
- [x] **Remove Serpentine Belt:** Use a 14mm socket to push the auto-tensioner, loosen the belt and pull it off. Let the tensioner return, then fully remove the belt.
- [x] **Passenger Wheel & Splash Shield:** Remove 5 lug nuts (19mm). Remove wheel. Remove the 10mm bolts at the corner of the bumper and inner fender fasteners. Pull the lower splash cover down and out.
- [x] **Peel Back Inner Fender Liner:** Remove the clips from the front of the inner fender liner (including one up top). Pull the liner back behind the brake rotor — this provides the clearance needed to access the timing covers.

#### Step 2: Remove Serpentine Belt Tensioner & Power Steering Pump
- [ ] **Serpentine Belt Tensioner:** Remove the center 14mm bolt (pull out as far as it goes), then the bottom 12mm bolt. Remove pulley and tensioner assembly.
- [ ] **Power Steering Pump:** Remove the 12mm bolt on the back and the 12mm bolt on the bottom. Lift the pump up and to the side — do NOT disconnect the hoses, just move it out of the way. Pull the PS reservoir straight up out of its bracket as well.

#### Step 3: Remove Timing Covers
- [ ] **Upper Front Cover:** Squeeze the back of the plastic harness clip and pull it out. Remove 10mm bolts (including one hiding next to the alternator). Remove cover.
- [ ] **Upper Rear Cover:** Remove 10mm bolts. Unhook the wire harness from the plastic stay on the cover. Remove cover.

#### Step 4: Set Timing to TDC (Before Removing Crank Pulley)
> [!WARNING] **Critical Sequence**
> You MUST set the engine to TDC *before* removing the crank pulley and belt. This ensures you have a reference point for reassembly. Only rotate the crankshaft **clockwise** — never counterclockwise.

- [ ] **Heat Crank Bolt:** Apply heat to the crank bolt — it has factory thread locker. This helps break it free.
- [ ] **Break Crank Bolt Free:** Use the Honda Crank Pulley Holder Tool + 19mm socket + breaker bar. *(Tip: Impact gun with a Lisle Heavy Mass 19mm socket also works.)* Crack the bolt free but do **not** fully remove it yet.
- [ ] **Identify Timing Marks:** On the crankshaft cog behind the pulley, find the small plate with an arrow — this is the block timing mark. On the crank gear, find the single tooth with a small **dimple** — that is the crank timing mark.
- [ ] **Mark with Paint:** Use white nail polish or a paint marker to highlight both the arrow on the block and the dimple on the crank gear so they're easily visible.
- [ ] **Rotate to TDC:** Reinstall the crank bolt finger-tight. Using a ratchet on the crank bolt, rotate the engine **clockwise only** until the painted dimple aligns perfectly with the arrow.
- [ ] **Verify Cam Marks:** Go up top. The front cam gear has an arrow that must line up with a groove at the top of the head. Check the rear cam gear — its mark must also line up with its corresponding groove. If the cams are not aligned, come back down and rotate the crank through another 360° and recheck.
- [ ] **Remove Crank Bolt & Pulley:** Once TDC is confirmed, fully remove the crank bolt. Wiggle the crank pulley back and forth while pulling outward to remove it from the shaft.

#### Step 5: Support Engine & Remove Engine Mount
- [ ] **Support Engine:** Place a floor jack with a block of wood under the oil pan. Apply slight upward pressure.
- [ ] **Remove Engine Mount:** Remove the 10mm harness stay bolt on top of the mount. Remove the two 14mm bolts from the top of the mount bracket, and the 14mm bolts from the bottom (very tight fit — break free with ratchet, then spin out by hand). Remove the mount.

#### Step 6: Remove Lower Timing Cover & Old Components
- [ ] **Lower Timing Cover:** Remove the 10mm bolts and pull off the lower cover.
- [ ] **Reinstall Crank Bolt Temporarily:** Thread the crank bolt back in finger-tight — you'll use this to hold the crank while removing the old tensioner hardware.
- [ ] **Compress & Lock Tensioner:** Thread a spare bolt (e.g., a battery tie-down pole) through the bracket hole above the tensioner. Screw it down until it contacts the tensioner, then turn only ¼ to ½ turn more. *Do not over-compress or you'll bend the bolt.*
- [ ] **Remove Idler Pulley:** 14mm bolt. Remove bolt and pulley.
- [ ] **Remove Timing Belt:** Pull the belt off starting from the right side, working left.
- [ ] **Remove Tensioner Pulley:** 14mm bolt. Remove bolt and pulley.
- [ ] **Remove Tensioner:** Unthread the spare compression bolt. Remove the two 10mm mounting bolts (alternate back and forth to loosen evenly). Remove tensioner.
- [x] **Remove Water Pump:** Handled inspection. 
> [!NOTE] **Skip Decision (2026-04-09)**
> Water pump was inspected and found to be relatively new (replaced at ~85k miles). Decided to skip replacement during this session to save OEM life, as it showed no signs of weeping or bearing play. New Aisin pump retained in spare parts inventory.

#### Step 7: Clean & Install Water Pump
- [x] **Inspection:** Surface cleaned, no leaks found. (Pump not replaced - see note above).

#### Step 8: Install New Timing Components
- [ ] **Install New Tensioner:** Mount the new hydraulic tensioner with the two 10mm bolts. **Torque to 9 ft-lbs.** Leave the tensioner pin installed for now.
- [ ] **Install Tensioner Adjuster Pulley:** Make sure to transfer the collar from the old pulley to the new one. Install onto the water pump stud. **Torque the 14mm bolt to 18 ft-lbs.**
- [ ] **Install Idler Pulley:** Clean old threads off the reused bolt and apply fresh **blue threadlocker**. The shouldered side of the pulley faces toward the engine. *(Check the Aisin instruction packet — if your VIN falls within the listed range, install the included shim with "OUT" stamped side facing you, placed under the pulley.)* **Torque the 14mm bolt to 33 ft-lbs.**

#### Step 9: Route New Timing Belt
> [!WARNING] **Belt Routing Order**
> Start at the **crank**, go up and over the **idler**, up to the **front cam**, down around the **water pump**, up to the **rear cam**, and finally over the **tensioner adjuster pulley**. Keep the belt taut on the drive side (non-tensioner side) at all times.

- [ ] **Crank Gear:** Slip the belt onto the crankshaft cog first. Ensure it seats perfectly in the teeth.
- [ ] **Idler → Front Cam:** Route over the idler pulley (keep taut!), then up and onto the front cam gear. You may need to work a tooth or two tighter — the belt must be snug on this side. **Watch your timing marks** as you work — if they shift, stop and correct.
- [ ] **Water Pump → Rear Cam → Tensioner:** Route around the water pump, up to the rear cam gear, and finally over the tensioner adjuster pulley.
- [ ] **Triple-Check All Timing Marks:** Verify the crank dimple aligns with the block arrow. Go up top and verify both front and rear cam marks are perfectly aligned in their grooves.
- [ ] **Pull Tensioner Pin:** Use pliers to remove the pin from the hydraulic tensioner. The tensioner will apply automatic pressure to the belt.
- [ ] **Final Tension Check:** Verify belt tightness. Go around and triple-check all three timing marks one more time.

#### Step 10: Reassemble
- [x] **Engine Mount Bracket:** Installed new engine mount (Passenger side).
- [x] **Engine Mount Rubber:** Adjust the jack up/down to align bolt holes. Thread in bolts. **Torque mount bolts to 32 ft-lbs**.
- [x] **Crank Plate & Lower Cover:** Remove the temporary crank bolt. Install the curved plate (curve facing out). Install the lower timing cover and snug down all 10mm bolts.
- [x] **Crank Bolt — Oil the Washer:** Clean the crank bolt and washer with brake cleaner and a rag. Apply fresh **engine oil** between the washer and the bolt head, and on top of the washer.
- [x] **Reinstall Crank Pulley:** Set it into the keyway, wiggle into place, and hand-thread the bolt. **Torque to 48 ft-lbs, then an additional 60°**.
- [x] **Upper Timing Covers:** Reinstall front and rear covers.
- [x] **Serpentine Belt Tensioner:** Reinstall tensioner with the large center bolt through the pulley. **Torque center pulley bolt to 33 ft-lbs. Torque bottom mounting bolt to 16 ft-lbs.**
- [x] **Power Steering Pump:** Set pump back into place. **Torque both bolts to 16 ft-lbs.** 
- [x] **Serpentine Belt:** Route belt around the crank and AC compressor.
- [x] **Coolant Reservoir & Battery:** Drop coolant reservoir into its bracket, reinstall the line into the plastic stay. Install battery. Reconnect **positive first, then negative.** Snug terminal bolts.
- [x] **Fender Liner & Wheel:** Push inner fender liner back into place. Reinstall wheel and **torque lug nuts to 80 ft-lbs in a star pattern.**

- [ ] **Reassemble Intake Manifold:** **Remove the cardboard/tape from the Injector Bases (Runners)!** Install the spacer plate and new manifold-to-base spacer gaskets. Lower the Intake Manifold (Plenum) onto the Injector Bases (Runners). Hand-thread the internal 10mm bolts.

## 3. First Start & Post-Repair Protocol (The "Smoke Test")
Before slamming the hood shut and calling it a day, run through this final checklist to ensure the major engine work was successful:

### The "Danger List" Check
These are the most commonly forgotten connectors on the J35. Verify they are all securely plugged in:
- [ ] The hidden bottom coolant hose on the Throttle Body.
- [ ] The Knock Sensor sub-harness (buried deep in the valley).
- [ ] The EGR valve electrical connector.
- [ ] The rear PCV vacuum hose (behind the manifold).

### System Checks
- [ ] **Reconnect Battery:** Reattach the negative terminal.
- [ ] **Visual Inspection:** Double-check all colored tape labels are matched up across electrical connectors, PCV, and vacuum hoses.
- [ ] **Start Engine & Listen:** Listen specifically for a "hissing" sound (indicates a vacuum leak from an improperly seated intake manifold gasket).
- [ ] **Check for Fluid Leaks:** Look under the car near the radiator (coolant) and around the rear cylinder head (oil).
- [ ] **Burp the Coolant:** Let the engine run with the **Lisle Spill-Free Funnel** firmly attached to the radiator neck. Ensure the cabin heater is blowing hot air to confirm all air bubbles are out.
- [ ] **Test Drive:** Clear any lingering OBD-II codes, then take a 15-minute test drive to verify the VTM-4 and Engine codes do not return.
