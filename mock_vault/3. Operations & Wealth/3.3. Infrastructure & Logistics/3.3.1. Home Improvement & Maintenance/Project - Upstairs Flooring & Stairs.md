# Project - Upstairs Flooring & Stairs

> [!abstract] Overview
> Complete renovation of the upstairs flooring and staircase (steps and risers). Following the removal of old carpeting, this project involves remediation of the concrete subfloor and installation of durable, high-traction flooring optimized for elderly safety and fall prevention.

## 👵 Critical Context: Elderly Safety & Fall Prevention
This project is for a home with elderly residents. The primary user (Mom) has a history of falls, making **safety, traction, and visibility** the highest priority constraints—trumping pure aesthetics.

### Key Accessibility Requirements:
- **Slip Resistance:** All hard surfaces must have a high **Coefficient of Friction (COF)** or an **R10/R11 slip rating**.
- **Impact Attenuation:** If a fall occurs, the material should provide some level of "forgiveness" (e.g., cork, rubber, or high-quality padded LVP).
- **Visual Contrast:** High-contrast nosing on stairs to clearly define the edge of each step.
- **Minimal Transitions:** Ensure completely flush transitions between rooms to prevent tripping on "lips" or T-moldings.

## 🏗️ Execution Plan & Options

### 1. Flooring Material Options (Safety-First)
| Option                    | Safety Rating | Pros                                                                        | Cons                                                                        |
| :------------------------ | :------------ | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Padded LVP (Textured)** | High          | Water-resistant, textured for grip, built-in underlayment for some cushion. | Still a hard surface; requires careful selection of "non-slip" texture.     |
| **Low-Pile Carpet**       | Excellent     | Best impact cushioning, high traction, quiet for upstairs.                  | Harder to clean; can snag on walkers if pile is too loose, traps allergens. |
| **Cork / Rubber**         | Superior      | Natural "give" Reduces joint pain and fall injury risk; naturally non-slip. | More niche aesthetic; rubber can look industrial.                           |

### 2. Senior-Safe Stair Renovation
- **Contrast Nosing:** Use a color for the stair edge (nosing) that contrasts sharply with the tread (e.g., dark wood with a lighter edge).
- **Dual Handrails:** Verify existing handrails are rock-solid. Consider adding a second rail to the opposite wall.
- **Traction Strips:** If using hard-surface treads, install integrated non-slip clear or color-matched abrasive strips.
- **Lighting:** Integrate motion-sensing LED "step lights" or ensure the overhead lighting is shadow-free.

### 3. Concrete Subfloor Remediation
- **Leveling:** Absolutely critical. Even a 1/4" dip can cause an LVP plank to "flex," which creates a subtle trip hazard.
- **Moisture Testing:** Perform calcium chloride or RH probe tests. Moisture under LVP can cause "tenting" at the seams—a major trip risk.

## 🧠 AGENT Recommendation & Rationale
**Primary Recommendation: High-Quality Textured LVP (Commercial Grade) with Integrated Padding.**

- **Why:** While carpet is softer, **Textured LVP** with an **R10/R11 slip rating** provides the best balance. It offers superior traction (doesn't get "slippery" as easily as wood), the integrated cork/foam padding provides impact attenuation to reduce injury from falls, and it is significantly easier to clean for elderly residents (no allergen/snagging issues).
- **Secondary Non-Negotiable:** **High-Contrast Nosing.** Do not rely on "matching" wood grain on the stairs; the edge of the step must be visually distinct for those with declining depth perception.

## 🔍 In-Depth Hiring & Vetting Guide

### The Search Strategy
- **CAPS Certification:** Look for contractors with a **Certified Aging-in-Place Specialist (CAPS)** designation. They are trained specifically in senior home safety.
- **Specific Stair Portfolios:** Ask to see photos of *stair* jobs. Many floorers are great at flats but struggle with the precision of stairs.
- **Cleanliness & Conduct:** Because parents are in the home, you need a crew that utilizes **HEPA dust extraction** and maintains a "no-smoking/low-noise" job site.

### Questions for the Deep-Dive Interview
1. **"Can you provide proof of both General Liability AND Workers' Comp insurance?"** (Essential to protect your parents from liability if a worker is hurt).
2. **"What specific slip-resistance rating (COF) does this material have?"** (If they don't know what COF is, they aren't the right specialist).
3. **"Do you use a 'dustless' installation process?"** (Vital for parents' respiratory health).
4. **"How do you handle the transition between the top step and the upstairs floor?"** (This is a common "lip" and trip point).
5. **"Will the same crew be here every day, or do you rotate subcontractors?"**

### Contract Must-Haves
- **Itemized Subfloor Prep:** Specify that "Leveling to [X] tolerance" is included.
- **Cleanup Cadence:** Daily removal of debris and vacuuming of the workspace.
- **Payment Schedule:** Never more than 33% upfront. Final 25% held until a "walk-through" ensures no trip hazards or loose nosing.
- **Timeline:** Explicit start and end dates to minimize the duration of "stair-outage" for parents.
