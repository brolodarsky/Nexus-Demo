### Description
A guide for transitioning a "Landline/VoIP" number (MagicJack) to Google Voice by "bridging" through a prepaid mobile carrier. This process is necessary because Google Voice only accepts "Mobile" numbers for porting.

### The "Landline" Problem (Why you can't just click 'Transfer')
Google Voice only accepts **"Mobile"** numbers. MagicJack is classified as a **"Landline/VoIP"** service. To get Google to accept it, you must **"bridge"** the number through a cheap prepaid mobile carrier first. This doesn't mean he has to use a cell phone forever—it’s just a temporary step to change the number's **"label"** in the national database.

### Step 1: The Number Move (The "Bridge" Method)
If Dad wants to keep his exact same phone number:
1. **Buy a Cheap SIM:** Get a $5–$10 prepaid SIM (like [Tello](https://tello.com) or [T-Mobile Prepaid](https://prepaid.t-mobile.com)). 
2. **Port to the SIM:** Get the necessary account details from [MagicJack](https://my.magicjack.com). Give those to the mobile carrier to move the number to that SIM card.
3. **The Switchover:** He keeps using MagicJack until it stops working. At that moment, his number is now on the mobile phone. 
4. **Wait 7 Days:** He must keep the number on the mobile phone for one week so the "Landline" label clears. **He will not lose his number during this time.**
5. **Final Move to Google:** Go to [voice.google.com](https://voice.google.com) > **Settings** > **Port a number**. Pay the one-time $20 fee. Once finished, calls ring on his laptop.

---

### Step 2: Moving the Contacts
1. **Export from MagicJack:** Log into his [MagicJack account](https://my.magicjack.com). Go to **Contacts** > **Export** (choose "Google CSV").
2. **Import to Google:** Go to [contacts.google.com](https://contacts.google.com). Click **Import** and upload that file.

---

### Step 3: Making the Laptop "Dad-Proof"
1. **Pin the Tab:** Open [voice.google.com](https://voice.google.com), **right-click** the tab at the very top, and select **Pin**. This shrinks it and prevents it from being closed by accident.
2. **Auto-Restart:** In Chrome/Edge Settings, go to **On Startup** and select **"Continue where you left off."** This ensures his "phone" opens automatically if the computer restarts.
3. **Power Settings:** Set the laptop to **"Never sleep when plugged in"** so he doesn't miss live calls while away from the desk. (If he does miss a call, it will still take a voicemail in the cloud!)
