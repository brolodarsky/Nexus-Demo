## Computer Science Grads Facing a Lack of Entry-Level Jobs and a Career Readiness Gap

Less than a decade ago, a computer science degree almost guaranteed employment for college graduates, but with AI reshaping industries, these grads are among those struggling the most to secure their first job.

### Entry-level hiring at an all-time low for computer science
Unemployment for recent computer science grads has reached 6.1% — well above the already-high graduate unemployment average of 4.8%. The U.S. is adding historically low levels of new jobs per month, and the **World Economic Forum** predicts that 22% of current jobs will be impacted by creation or elimination over the next five years.

### Employers Raising the Bar
With a decline in available roles, alongside an increase in candidates and shifting skills requirements, hiring power is in the hands of employers. There is an emphasis on:
- Job-specific technical skills.
- Alignment with fast-paced industry changes.
- Industry-aligned skills.

### Preparedness and Instruction
- Computing and tech instructors (40%) are the second least likely to say that their students are sufficiently prepared to enter the workforce.
- The rapid pace of AI enhancements makes it difficult for instruction to keep up.
- One in five students say their education program did nothing to help foster critical job-securing personal connections.

### Industry Outlook: Where to go from here?
- 89% of computing and tech instructors feel that students need experience using **Gen AI** before they graduate.
- There is a need for closer alignment between educators, institutions, and employers to ensure students are equipped with relevant skills.
- Recognizing the need for adaptation is the first step toward long-term post-graduation success.
