## How Computer Science Grads Can Find Career Success In The Era Of AI.

Students across the country have been warned that there are no six-figure starting salaries in Big Tech anymore. In this era of economic contraction and AI expansion, there aren’t even enough entry-level jobs to go around.

### Challenges:
- Silicon Valley misled young people for the past decade, telling them that studying computer science would be their golden ticket to a high-paying job.
- Undergraduate enrollment in computer science doubled from 2014 to 2024.
- Recent CS graduates face high unemployment rates (~6.1%).

### Three No-Regrets Steps for Success:

1. **Adopt a Flexible Mindset:** The six-figure starting job in a Big Tech firm is unlikely to materialize. Accepting this is an essential step toward developing the flexible mindset you need to pivot and leverage your skills.
2. **Gain Practical AI Fluency:** Identify skills employers seek for advanced tech positions. Develop judgment and critical thinking, not just technical skills. Seek internships, work-based learning, or project-based work with feedback.
3. **Broaden Your Job Search:** Big Tech is not the only industry. Consider adjacent sectors like healthcare, health tech, public health, and government tech.

### The Real Promise of Higher Education:
Education isn't a stock to be sold when the market dips. A CS degree helps young people troubleshoot, problem-solve, and think critically. Its value plays out over a lifetime, not just in the single moment a graduate enters the market.

**Conclusion:** It’s not that entry-level computer science jobs are gone forever; they’re evolving. And you can, too.
