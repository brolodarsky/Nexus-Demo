# The Job Hunt War Room: CS Roles & Target Profiles

## 1. The "Sweet Spot" Roles
To bypass the entry-level SWE grind, focus on roles that require **Orchestration** and **Human-in-the-Loop** judgment.

> [!TIP]
> **Complete Taxonomy:** See the exhaustive [[Synthesis - AI Superagency & The Future of Work#5. Taxonomy of Agentic Roles (2026+)|Taxonomy of Agentic Roles]] for a list of 16+ specific career paths.

### Core Target Categories:
*   **Agentic Engineering & Orchestration:** Building the logic (LangGraph, State Machines) and retrieval ([[Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG)|RAG]]) systems that power superagency.
*   **Cognitive Infrastructure & MLOps:** Building the "scaffolding" (vLLM, Vector DBs, MCP integrations) that runs the digital workforce.
*   **Evaluation, Trust & Safety:** Leveraging your **AI Rating experience** to build benchmarks, fine-tune models (RLHF/SFT), and red-team agentic systems.
*   **Strategic & Customer-Centric (Bypass Roles):** AI Solutions Architecture, Solutions Engineering, and Technical Product Management. These value your technical intuition but face significantly less competition than standard "Software Engineer" titles.
*   **Domain Verticals (Healthcare & Legal):** High-value automation for complex medical reasoning (Nexus.0 focus) or contract intelligence.

## 3. Strategy for the Hunt
* **Bypassing the Experience Trap ("Degree + Portfolio + Real Experience"):** Traditional application pipeline is dead. 110k CS grads fighting for fewer entry-level spots, academic coursework is invisible. The new standard is *live, deployed, real-world utility*.
* **The "Freedom Fund" Leverage (🛑 Status: Ended):** The AI rating job that previously funded the Freedom Fund has officially ended as of May 2026. With ~$2k remaining and no active income, the financial runway is limited but not critical (living at home). **The strategic implication is unchanged:** do not take a highly-exposed routine role out of panic. Target roles offering "Superagency." **The tactical implication is now critical:** the income bridge must be activated from alternative sources (CS tutoring, freelance automation) immediately to preserve the remaining $2k. See [[Income Streams Hub]] for the current income status.
* **Portfolio Pivot:** Reposition [[Project - Nexus Agentic Engine|Nexus Agentic Engine]] as your primary talking point. Describe them as "Agentic Workflows," "Proprietary Context Engines," and *deployed* software. Treat them as real-world products showing true AI fluency, rather than just homework assignments. (See [[Portfolio Hub]] for exact elevator pitches and resume bullet points).
* **Human-Centricity, AI Fluency & Systems Thinking:** Highlight your ability to manage non-deterministic AI outputs and design complex systems over pure syntax knowledge. Demonstrate that you are an "Agent Orchestrator" rather than an easily automated task executor.
* **Architect vs. Platform User:** As AI companies release "Agent Builders" (OpenAI, Google, Microsoft), distinguish yourself by mastering the **underlying orchestration**. Companies don't just need people who can type prompts into a builder; they need architects who can build custom, deterministic, and safe state machines using frameworks like **LangGraph**. Position yourself as the one who builds the engine, not just someone who turns the key.
* **The "Black Hole" Warning:** Stop using LinkedIn/Indeed for mass applications. These are structurally designed for AI-shredding and high-volume noise. Your Dec 2025 graduation means you must use **University Pipelines (Handshake)** or **Direct Outreach** to EMs/Founders.
* **The "Lights-On" Career:** Look for roles that offer a clear path to **remote or hybrid work**. This supports your [[Goals|long-term goal]] of having your own space while maintaining a high-income trajectory in the AI era.

### B. The "Sniper" Protocol (Direct Outreach)
Instead of applying on a portal, use this 3-step loop for every target company:
1.  **Identify the EM/Founder/PI:** Find the Engineering Manager, Founding Engineer, or Principal Investigator (PI) on LinkedIn or lab websites.
2.  **The Transition Narrative:** Send a tailored DM/Email bridging your **AI Rating experience** (Safety/Eval/non-determinism) to their specific product challenge.
3.  **Proof of Work:** Always include a direct link to a *deployed* project (Feeder or Nexus.0).

**Core principle:** The DM is the lowest bar. The founders who respond are ones who already know who you are. **Make them aware of you first — then the DM is a follow-up, not a cold open.**

#### Pre-Outreach Moves (Do at least one before DMing)
- [ ] **Build something for them** — a micro-tool, a data insight, a demo agent in their domain. Send it with the DM.
- [ ] **Post a public product teardown** — a LinkedIn post or short article critiquing their product thoughtfully. Tag the founder. The DM references the post.
- [ ] **[[Project - Nexus Architecture Loom Demo|Record a Loom]]** — 2-3 min screen recording walking through a UX issue or feature idea. Far harder to ignore than text.
- [ ] **Engage their content for weeks first** — leave smart comments on their posts before you DM. By the time you reach out, you're already a familiar name.
- [ ] **Contribute to their open source** — submit a real PR to their public repo. Signals you actually looked at their codebase.
- [ ] **Get a weak-tie intro** — even a mutual acquaintance's one-liner email turns a cold DM into a warm one.
