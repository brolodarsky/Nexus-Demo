### How to Evaluate an Offer
1. **Total Compensation (TC):** Base + Bonus + Equity (annualized) + Benefits value.
2. **Effective Hourly Rate:** TC ÷ (expected weekly hours × 52). A $150K role at 60 hrs/week ≈ $48/hr — barely above your AI rating gig.
3. **Growth Trajectory:** Will this role make you more valuable in 2 years? Check against [[Job Hunt War Room#1. The "Sweet Spot" Roles|target role list]].
4. **Intangibles:** Remote flexibility, team quality, learning budget, manager reputation.

## Equity 101

### RSUs (Restricted Stock Units)
- **What:** Shares granted that vest over time (typically 4-year schedule with 1-year cliff).
- **Vesting Cliff:** You get nothing if you leave before the cliff (usually 12 months).
- **Tax:** Taxed as ordinary income upon vesting. Set aside ~30-40% for taxes.
- **Public vs. Private:** Public company RSUs have a known value. Private company RSUs are speculative — discount them 50–80% in your mental math.

### Stock Options (ISOs / NSOs)
- **ISOs (Incentive Stock Options):** Tax-advantaged but complex. Exercise early if possible (83(b) election).
- **NSOs (Non-Qualified Stock Options):** Taxed as ordinary income on exercise. Simpler but more expensive.
- **Strike Price:** What you pay to buy the shares. Only valuable if company value > strike price.
- **Exercise Window:** Most companies give 90 days post-departure to exercise. Negotiate for longer (1–2 years).

### What to Negotiate on Equity
- **More shares** (always ask — this is the easiest "yes" for companies).
- **Accelerated vesting** on acquisition or layoff (double-trigger acceleration).
- **Extended exercise window** (critical if you leave a startup before IPO).

## Negotiation Scripts

### The Counter-Offer Framework
> "Thank you for the offer — I'm genuinely excited about the role and the team. Based on my research and competing conversations, I was targeting a base in the range of $[X–Y]. Is there flexibility to close that gap?"

**Key principles:**
- **Always give a range**, not a single number. Anchor high.
- **Never lie** about competing offers, but you can say "competing conversations" even if they're early-stage.
- **Negotiate base first**, then equity, then signing bonus, then everything else.

### Handling "That's Our Best Offer"
> "I understand budget constraints. Could we explore other dimensions — a signing bonus, additional equity, a guaranteed review at 6 months, or a professional development budget?"

### Handling Exploding Offers
> "I appreciate the timeline, but I want to make a decision I'm fully committed to. Could we extend the deadline by [3–5 business days]? I want to make sure I'm choosing [Company] for the right reasons, not under pressure."

### Handling "What's Your Current Salary?"
> "I'd prefer to focus on the value I'll bring to this role rather than anchor to my current compensation. Based on my research, the market range for this role is $[X–Y]."

- **Note:** In NYC, it is illegal for employers to ask about salary history ([NYC Pay Transparency Law](https://www.nyc.gov/site/cchr/media/pay-transparency.page)).
