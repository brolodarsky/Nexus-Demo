## Role Overview

- **Company:** Abridge
- **Team:** BuilderScience (GenAI Engineering)
- **Location:** San Francisco, CA; New York, NY; Pittsburgh, PA (Hybrid — minimum 3 days/week in office)
- **Type:** Full-time
- **Compensation:** $255,000 – $300,000 base + Equity

Abridge is a high-growth startup purpose-built for medical conversations, transforming patient-clinician dialogues into structured clinical notes in real-time. This role is focused on translating cutting-edge language model capabilities into dependable, real-world clinical systems.

---

## What You'll Do

- **Design and build GenAI systems** that turn LLMs into composable, dependable tools—leveraging retrieval, tool use, agentic reasoning, and structured outputs.
- Collaborate with ML and infra engineers to **scale and optimize GenAI workflows**, managing latency, context windows, and model choice.
- **Write high-quality, modular code** that’s graceful under failure, flexible to change, and easy to iterate on.
- **Own major architectural decisions**—how we architect workflows, define data flow, cache intermediate state, and structure generative outputs.
- **Drive rigorous evaluation**: build benchmark datasets, develop automated and human-in-the-loop frameworks, design experiments to surface failure modes and edge cases, run A/B tests to inform deployment, and distill insights from clinician feedback to evaluate and guide model improvement.
- **Leverage frontier capabilities**: rapidly prototype with new models and model capabilities, open-source tools, and novel prompting techniques.

---

## What You'll Bring

- **GenAI / LLM Experience:** 1–2+ years focused on GenAI or LLM-powered products, with 3+ years of production-grade systems experience.
- **Orchestration & Tooling:** Deep fluency with LLM APIs, prompting strategies, and orchestration patterns (e.g., LangChain, LlamaIndex, custom pipelines).
- **Retrieval & Tool Use:** Experience with retrieval systems (semantic and lexical retrieval, vector DBs, efficient kNN), function calling, tool-use, or agentic workflows.
- **Model Evaluation:** Working knowledge of model evaluation, building diverse datasets, conducting automated and human-in-the-loop evaluations, and running A/B tests.
- **Core Engineering:** Strong Python fundamentals—including writing clean code, designing comprehensive test-cases, and familiarity with core language features; experience with async programming, performance profiling, packaging, and deployment is strongly preferred.
- **Execution Mentality:** Hybrid office model (SF, NYC, or Pittsburgh). Good taste and intuition: knowing when to move fast, ship, and iterate vs. taking a beat to tackle tech debt.
